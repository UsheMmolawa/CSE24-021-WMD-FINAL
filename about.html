About Warner Bros.

A century of storytelling excellence

Our Story

Warner Bros. is a globally recognized entertainment company, known for producing some of the most iconic films and TV shows in history.

Founded in 1923, Warner Bros. has brought to life legendary characters, unforgettable stories, and groundbreaking entertainment experiences.

With a commitment to creativity and innovation, Warner Bros. continues to be a leader in the entertainment industry, shaping the future of film and television.

Our History

1923

Warner Bros. is founded by brothers Harry, Albert, Sam, and Jack Warner. The company begins as a small production studio in Hollywood.

1927

Release of "The Jazz Singer," the first feature-length motion picture with synchronized dialogue sequences, revolutionizing the film industry.

1930s-1940s

The Golden Age of Warner Bros. with iconic gangster films, musicals, and the introduction of beloved characters like Bugs Bunny.

1960s-1970s

Expansion into television production with hits like "The Flintstones" and "Batman," while continuing successful film productions.

1989

Launch of the modern Batman franchise with Tim Burton's "Batman," starring Michael Keaton and Jack Nicholson.

2001

Beginning of the Harry Potter film series, which would become one of the most successful franchises in history.

Present Day

Continuing to produce blockbuster films and hit TV shows while expanding into streaming services and global markets.
